Effect of different levels of g-force on the cell density growth of Chlamydomonas moewusii

Henry Cantor, HTHS

Research Paper
Research Poster

Abstract

  • Study conducted to determine the effect that levels of hypergravity between 1000 and 3000 x g has on the growth of algae
  • Hypothesis was that with each incremental increase in hypergravity, the growth of the algae would decrease due to cell damage and possibly death
  • Number of cells in the corner squares of a hemocytometer counted at the beginning of each trial
  • Cells incubated in centrifuge, grown for two weeks, then counted again
  • Before and after cell densities analyzed with a logarithmic equation and measured in the number of divisions per cell
  • Found that the control had a significantly higher growth compared to the experimental groups, but the growth of these were not found to be significantly different from each other

Background

  • Space travel and other environments of high g-force are becoming an increasingly large part of the world
  • Algae is growing in its potential as source of food and vitamins in addition to biofuel
  • Chlamydomonas moewusii was subjected to g-force in this study and analyzed to determine the effect the g-force had on algae
  • Previous studies analyzed the effect of g-force on bacteria and the effect of different factors like microgravity and plastics on algae

Rationale

  • Study strives to provide novel, meaningful, and relevant data in understanding hypergravity on cell growth
  • Change in cell density after incubation with periods of varying degrees of centrifugation (or lack thereof for the control) hopefully provides info on a specific and little-studies area of research
  • Information on how algae may behave outside of Earth was collected, as conditions of the current study mimic high-gravity environments
