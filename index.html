Effect of different levels of g-force on the cell density growth of Chlamydomonas moewusii

Henry Cantor and Mr. Craig Queenan, HTHS

Research Paper
Research Poster

Abstract

The study was conducted to determine the effect that levels of hypergravity between 1000 and 3000 x g has on the growth of algae. The hypothesis was that with each incremental increase in hypergravity, the growth of the algae would decrease due to cell damage and possibly death. The number of cells in the corner squares of a hemocytometer was counted at the beginning of each trial. Cells were incubated and exposed to high g-force and then left without further centrifugation overnight. After two weeks, they were counted a second time. The before and after cell densities were analyzed with a logarithmic equation and measured in the number of divisions per cell. The data was graphed, and a single factor ANOVA test was performed, followed by t-tests. Ultimately, it was found that the Control had a significantly higher growth compared to the experimental groups, but the growth of these were not found to be significantly different from each other.
